# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners own and communicate the product vision, working closely with the project manager on backlog prioritization and serving as the primary liaison with business stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Own and communicate product vision and strategy
- Collaborate with project manager on backlog prioritization
- Serve as primary liaison with business stakeholders
- Define and clarify user stories and acceptance criteria
- Make trade-off decisions on scope and features
- Validate that delivered features meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure the product delivers maximum business value
- Maintain clear alignment between stakeholder needs and development priorities
- Balance competing demands while maintaining product coherence
- Foster strong collaboration between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Regular backlog refinement sessions with project manager
- Stakeholder reviews and feedback sessions
- Sprint planning to clarify priorities and acceptance criteria
- Feature demos and validation with business users

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on backlog prioritization, timeline planning, and stakeholder communication
- **Business Analyst**: Partners on requirements gathering and documentation
- **Tech Lead**: Discusses technical feasibility and trade-offs
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on product vision and roadmap direction

---

## Quality Advocate

### Role Summary
Quality Advocates champion quality practices throughout the project lifecycle, from planning through release, ensuring quality is built into every phase rather than just tested at the end.

### Responsibilities
- Advocate for quality practices and standards across all project phases
- Coordinate quality initiatives with development and QA/testing teams
- Review and provide feedback on process improvements
- Identify quality risks early and propose mitigations
- Promote test automation and quality metrics
- Facilitate quality-related discussions and retrospectives

### Goals
- Foster a culture where quality is everyone's responsibility
- Reduce defects and rework through proactive quality practices
- Increase test coverage and automation
- Ensure quality considerations are included in planning and design

### Typical Communication
- Quality reviews during sprint planning and design sessions
- Regular sync with QA/testers on testing strategy and coverage
- Process improvement suggestions in retrospectives
- Quality metrics and trend reporting

### Key Interactions
- **QA/Testers**: Partners closely on testing strategy, automation, and quality metrics
- **Developers**: Collaborates on code quality, testing practices, and technical debt
- **Project Manager**: Provides input on quality risks and mitigation strategies
- **Product Owner**: Advises on quality trade-offs and acceptance criteria

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads develop and execute communication plans, ensuring consistent messaging to all audiences and managing both internal team communications and external stakeholder engagement.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute comprehensive communication plans
- Ensure consistent messaging across all project communications
- Interface with stakeholders and project team members
- Manage communication channels and cadence
- Coordinate announcements, updates, and change notifications
- Track and respond to stakeholder questions and concerns

### Goals
- Maintain clear, timely, and transparent communication
- Ensure all stakeholders are appropriately informed
- Reduce miscommunication and information gaps
- Build trust through consistent and reliable updates

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder updates and newsletters
- Communication plan reviews with project manager
- Coordination meetings with all team members for input
- Feedback sessions to improve communication effectiveness

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on communication strategy, timing, and content
- **Product Owner**: Ensures business messaging aligns with product vision
- **All Team Members**: Gathers input and status updates for stakeholder communications
- **Stakeholders**: Manages direct communications and feedback loops

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers manage and document change requests, reviewing impact and ensuring team alignment while working with all delivery roles to execute change protocols effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Manage and document all change requests
- Review and assess impact of proposed changes
- Ensure team alignment on change decisions
- Work with delivery roles to execute change protocols
- Track change history and communicate status
- Coordinate change review boards and approval processes

### Goals
- Ensure changes are evaluated systematically and transparently
- Minimize disruption from uncontrolled changes
- Maintain clear audit trail of all changes
- Balance flexibility with stability and predictability

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews and impact assessments
- Change advisory board meetings
- Status updates on pending and approved changes
- Post-implementation reviews and lessons learned

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on change impact, timeline adjustments, and risk management
- **Product Owner**: Reviews change requests against product vision and priorities
- **Development/QA**: Collaborates on technical impact assessment and implementation
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates change status, impacts, and approvals
